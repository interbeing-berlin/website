## Organization & Team

### Ecosystem

#### Sunday services
The heart of church of interbeing events, the interbeing service occurs every Sunday, usually at 13:00 at the Genezarethkirche, Herrfurthplatz 14. Services are open to the public, and supported by donations. Services last 1.5 - 2 hours, and usually include singing, meditation, movement or embodiment practices, dialogue, and elements of ritual. Services are guided by volunteer hosts from the community, and usually follow a theme. 

Services occasionally occur in other locations and at slightly different times depending on the availability of the church space.

Service themes and location details are posted in the [updates telegram group](https://t.me/+4_DIFOg_CeFiN2M8) and on [instagram](https://instagram.com/church_of_interbeing).

We value your feedback on services! Please message the host, hosting steward, or council, or fill in [this form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSc_t4marCcJB8Dj4JeQ_E6aZr0cCkaoVGC_k3rTmBg09oustQ/viewform) to help us learn.

#### Being Lab
The Being Lab is a Tuesday evening workshop space that happens in a venue a few blocks from the church (the Gemeindesaal, Schillerpromenade 17 -- also provided by our amazing partners at [Startbahn](https://startbahn.berlin))

More details in the [Being Lab chat](https://t.me/thebeinglab).

The being lab is coordinated by Florencia Noriega and a team of volunteers.


#### Chanting Evenings
On the first Sunday of every month there is an evening of singing at the church, often following themes that reflect the cycle of the seasons. Coordination for Evening Chants is lead by Theresa Leisgang, Antonia Glugla, and Ronja Polzin. If you're interested participating in hosting and co-creating a chanting evening, get in touch!

Invitations are posted in [updates telegram group](https://t.me/+4_DIFOg_CeFiN2M8).

7:30am - 9am @ Genezareth Kirche

The choir, led by Antonia Glugla, meets every Thursday 7pm - 9pm at the Gemeindesaal. More info in the [telegram group](https://t.me/+hahLqAIThVFkYWI6).

The core stewards each care for a particular domain of the project's operations, and collectively hold the operational responsibility for whatever doesn't fall into these domains. Stewards are each paid a small stipend (between 200 and 400 euros monthly).

#### Conflict Awareness Team
The CAT is responsible for handling conflicts and any violations of the Church of Interbeing [Code of Conduct](https://interbeings.notion.site/Code-of-Conduct-v0-3-Public-967171a6813342fcb7ce602fbcf9d526). The Conflict Awareness Team lead is Carolin Goethel. 

If you've witnessed or been afffected by something that might be a violation of the code of conduct, or if there's a conflict that needs tending, you can report it (optionally anonymously) through the [reporting form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeyq_N1fvlCTSbOmFryz13rjk-8fAUMZSCPVbERF3NjDz-cXQ/viewform).
